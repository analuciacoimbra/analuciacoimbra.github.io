Hello! I'm Ana Lúcia and welcome to my portfolio!

I started as an occupational therapist. Then I spent years inside healthcare tech, bridging clinical users and development teams. Now I do UX Research and that entire journey shows up in how I work.

I observe how people actually behave, find the workarounds they no longer notice, and uncover what they genuinely need versus what they think they need. My research combines qualitative and quantitative methods and is designed to be meaningful, actionable, and helpful, whether that means informing a product roadmap, shaping a service or reframing how a team approaches a problem entirely.

How do people prepare their grocery shopping?

A comprehensive generative research study conducted in 2024/25 as a UX Researcher to understand user behaviours, needs and pain points in grocery shopping preparation. The research explored how people decide where to shop, manage budgets and leverage promotions. Findings reframed the product strategy and informed the development roadmap for a tool designed to help users save money during grocery shopping.

How do therapists manage their daily practice?

A generative study I carried out during my participation in the "Empreende Já" initiative (an entrepreneurship programme) in 2017, which allowed me to better understand the pains and needs of therapists. This study was used as the basis for the business plan presented at the end of the programme under the project name "Inspiring Care". This case study also includes a current reflection on what I would approach differently today.
